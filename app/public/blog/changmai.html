I was in the Hyperbolic Time Chamber called Chiang Mai🇹🇭

January 11, 2025

Hey guys, I was in Chiang Mai for 2 months and spent a great time there. I want to share what I did during my stay.

Why I was in Chiang Mai?

Right before arriving in Chiang Mai, I was in Australia. My visa was about to expire in 3 months, and I didn’t want to go back to Japan.

Pedro, a friend I met in Australia, told me, “South Asia, especially Thailand, is awesome. You should go there too.”

I asked him, “Alright, but which area do you recommend? If I go, I’ll need a decent co-working space and a gym.” He replied, “Then go to Chiang Mai.”

Planning

My plan in Chiang Mai was to create one or two of my own products to add to my software engineering portfolio. I wanted to ensure I could get a job when I returned to Japan.

I didn’t want to make a product just for my portfolio. Since building a product takes 2-5 months for me, I wanted to create something useful for myself, not just a project to show that I can code.

I already had a product, travelpackhub.com, but it didn’t have active users. I wanted to build more interactive apps that engage users, which are more challenging and would enhance my coding skills.

Hyperbolic Time Chamber

I called this 2 month trip “doing Hyperbolic Time Chamber but Chiang Mai.” My routine was simple: wake up, grab coffee, go to the gym, work at a co-working space, have dinner, and smoke weed with hostel mates. I repeated this every day without taking a break.

Staying busy gave me purpose. It’s good for mental health, as it helps avoid wasting energy on social media or other distractions.

Although the product I made didn’t make any money, I’m proud that I consistently worked on something every day.

Result

  • Created an app using AI-generated images: thisjobdoesnotexistyet.com
  • Built an English study app: eigopencil.com
  • Made a simple portfolio: kazumawada.com
  • Wrote a hostel diary: note.com/imnotkatsuma
  • Went to the gym almost every day
  • Practiced Muay Thai
